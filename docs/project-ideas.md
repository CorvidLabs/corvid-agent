# Project Ideas

Don't know what to ask your agent to build? Here are some ideas, organized by difficulty. Just paste any of these into a chat session.

---

## Beginner — Simple apps (minutes)

> "Build a personal portfolio website with my name, a bio section, and links to my projects"

> "Create a countdown timer to my birthday on [date]"

> "Make a simple calculator that works in the browser"

> "Build a random quote generator that shows a new quote when I click a button"

> "Create a color palette generator — click a button, get 5 random harmonious colors"

> "Build a unit converter (temperature, weight, distance)"

> "Make a Markdown previewer — I type on the left, see the result on the right"

> "Create a tip calculator for splitting bills at restaurants"

---

## Intermediate — Useful tools (minutes to hours)

> "Build a todo list app with categories, due dates, and local storage so nothing is lost"

> "Create a habit tracker that shows daily streaks and weekly progress charts"

> "Make a budget tracker where I enter expenses, categorize them, and see charts"

> "Build a recipe organizer — I paste in recipes, it saves them, I can search and filter"

> "Create a workout log where I track exercises, sets, reps, and see progress over time"

> "Build a bookmark manager with tags, search, and import/export"

> "Make a meeting notes app with timestamps, action items, and search"

> "Create a flashcard study app with spaced repetition"

---

## Advanced — Full applications (hours)

> "Build a weather dashboard using the Open-Meteo API — show current conditions, hourly and weekly forecasts, and air quality"

> "Create a personal blog with markdown posts, tags, a search bar, and an RSS feed"

> "Build a project management board like Trello — columns, draggable cards, labels, and due dates"

> "Make a file organizer that watches a folder and automatically sorts files by type and date"

> "Create a personal finance dashboard that reads CSV bank statements and shows spending trends"

> "Build a link shortener with click tracking and analytics"

> "Make a CI/CD dashboard that shows the status of all my GitHub repos' workflows"

---

## For your business

> "Review all open pull requests on [my-org/my-repo] and leave constructive feedback"

> "Write unit tests for the untested files in [my-org/my-repo]"

> "Set up a schedule to review PRs every morning at 9am"

> "Triage the open issues on [my-org/my-repo] — label them, prioritize, and suggest assignees"

> "Fix the failing CI build on [my-org/my-repo] branch [branch-name]"

> "Refactor [file-path] to be more readable and add documentation"

> "Create a README for [my-org/my-repo] based on the code"

---

## Tips

- **Be specific.** "Build a weather app" works, but "Build a weather app that shows the 5-day forecast with hourly temperatures and a rain probability chart" gets better results.
- **Iterate.** Start simple, then ask for changes: "Now add dark mode" or "Make the charts interactive."
- **Point it at your code.** "Look at my-org/my-repo and suggest improvements" is a great way to start.
- **Use schedules.** Set up daily PR reviews or weekly code quality checks to run automatically.
